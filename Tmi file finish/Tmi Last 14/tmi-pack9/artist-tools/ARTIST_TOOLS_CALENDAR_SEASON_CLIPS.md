# CONTENT_CALENDAR.md
## How Shows and Events Are Scheduled Across the Platform

---

## PLATFORM CONTENT CALENDAR

The platform has a master calendar that prevents:
- Two major events at the same time
- Monthly Idol rounds overlapping
- World Concerts competing with premieres

---

## CALENDAR TIERS

| Priority | Event Type | Managed By |
|---|---|---|
| P1 (exclusive) | Monthly Idol finals | Platform schedule |
| P1 (exclusive) | World Concert | Platform schedule |
| P2 (preferred) | Premieres | Artist + Platform |
| P3 (coordinated) | Artist shows | Artist + Platform |
| P4 (open) | Going Live | Artist at will |

---

## SCHEDULING RULES

- Artists can go live anytime (P4)
- Shows must be scheduled 48h+ in advance
- Premieres must be scheduled 7 days in advance
- Monthly Idol rounds are set by platform (never moved)
- World Concerts set by platform (4 weeks notice)
- No two P1 events can overlap

---

## ARTIST SCHEDULING FLOW

```
Artist selects "Schedule Event"
    ↓
Picks: event type, date/time, venue
    ↓
Platform checks calendar for conflicts
    ↓
  No conflict → Confirmed + calendar entry
  Conflict → Suggests alternative times
    ↓
Calendar syncs to:
  - Artist dashboard
  - Fan notifications
  - Platform event feed
  - Big Ace calendar view
```

# SEASON_ENGINE.md
## How Monthly Idol Cycles Work Season to Season

---

## SEASON STRUCTURE

Monthly Idol runs in seasons:
- **Season length**: 12 weeks
- **Rounds per season**: 4 (3 weeks each, 1 week finale)
- **Genres per season**: Platform selects each season
- **Winners**: 1 winner per genre track per season

---

## SEASON TIMELINE

```
WEEK 1–3: Round 1 (Open submissions)
WEEK 4–6: Round 2 (Top 50 per track advance)
WEEK 7–9: Round 3 (Top 20 per track advance)
WEEK 10–11: Finale (Top 5 per track compete)
WEEK 12: Winner announcement + Season close
    ↓ 1 week break
NEXT SEASON BEGINS
```

---

## SEASON HOSTS

Hosts rotate by season:
- **Monthly Idol core hosts**: Meridicus James, Aiko Starling, Zahra Voss
- Host lineup confirmed per season
- At least 2 of 3 must be available per season
- If 1 unavailable: rotation host covers

---

## SEASON WINNER BENEFITS

The Monthly Idol winner gets:
- Featured placement in magazine (homepage position)
- Automatic rank# boost (fast-tracked up index)
- Winner badge on profile (permanent, season-dated)
- Exclusive winner interview article (published)
- Access to Signature-tier venue for 30 days
- Cash prize (amount set per season)
- Trophy in their VEX wardrobe (unique season item)

---

## INTER-SEASON RULES

Between seasons:
- Replay access to past season content
- "Best of" editorial content
- Battle and cypher events continue (not affected by season)
- Going Live always available
- Monthly Idol panel hosts appear in rotation events

# CLIP_HIGHLIGHT_SYSTEM.md
## Auto-Generated Clips from Every Live Event

---

## PURPOSE
Every peak moment in a live event becomes a shareable clip automatically.
Artists don't need to do anything. It just happens.

---

## CLIP TRIGGER EVENTS

A clip is generated when:
- Crowd energy hits 90+ for 10+ seconds
- Major tip received ($50+)
- Winner announced
- Tier upgrade happens
- Artist-triggered spotlight moment
- Unusual reaction spike (gasp/cheer burst)

---

## CLIP FORMAT

Each auto-clip:
- 15–60 seconds long
- Starts 5 seconds before trigger
- Ends 10 seconds after peak resolves
- Branded with TMI frame overlay
- Artist name + event type label
- Timestamp + venue name

---

## CLIP DESTINATIONS

Clips are available in:
- Artist's Clip Center (private first)
- Artist can approve for public
- Shareable to: Instagram, TikTok, Twitter/X, YouTube Shorts
- Platform's own clip feed (with artist approval)

---

## ARTIST CLIP CONTROLS

Artist can:
- Approve or reject each auto-clip
- Trim clip (±10 seconds)
- Add caption/hashtags
- Share directly from dashboard
- Delete permanently

---

## FAN CLIP REQUESTS

Fans can also request a clip of a specific moment:
- Tap "Clip This" during event at any moment
- System generates clip of that moment (+/- 20s)
- Sent to artist for approval
- If approved: shared as fan-credited clip
