## SPONSORSHIP vs ADVERTISING DISTINCTION

Sponsorship is deeper than advertising:
- Sponsor aligns with a specific artist, event, room, or editorial feature
- Sponsor branding appears alongside artist identity
- Sponsors have campaign dashboards with analytics
- Sponsors can co-brand events (e.g., "Nike Presents: The Battle")
- Sponsor relationships are curated and approved

---

## SPONSORSHIP TYPES

| Type | What Is Sponsored | Sponsor Visibility |
|---|---|---|
| Artist Sponsorship | Artist profile + rooms | Sponsor logo on artist profile, rooms |
| Event Sponsorship | Battle, cypher, premiere | Sponsor banner + verbal mention |
| Venue Sponsorship | Venue digital twin | Sponsor board in venue rooms |
| Editorial Sponsorship | Magazine feature/article | Branded article header |
| Season Sponsorship | Full ranking season | Title sponsor in all season materials |
| Prize Sponsorship | Specific prize/reward | Prize card shows sponsor branding |

## SPONSOR CAMPAIGN WORKFLOW

```
1. Sponsor submits campaign request
2. Big Ace reviews and approves
3. Sponsor assets uploaded to R2 (approved format)
4. Campaign configured: dates, placements, artist alignment
5. Campaign goes live per feature flag
6. Sponsor receives analytics dashboard
7. Campaign ends → recap report generated
```

---

## SPONSOR PLACEMENT RULES

```
ALWAYS:
✓ Clearly labeled "Sponsored by [Brand]"
✓ Sponsor sees real analytics (impressions, clicks, engagement)
✓ Sponsor branding removed if contract ends

NEVER:
✗ Sponsor content during crown transfer
✗ Sponsor content during winner reveal
✗ Sponsor content during tier upgrade animation
✗ Sponsor audio autoplay in rooms
✗ Sponsor covering performer's face
✗ Sponsor placement without Big Ace approval
```

---

## SPONSOR ANALYTICS (Available in Sponsor Dashboard)

- Impressions per placement
- Click-through rate
- Room attendance during sponsor event
- Viewer demographics (aggregated, anonymized)
- Engagement rate (reactions during sponsor content)
- Artist growth metrics during campaign period
