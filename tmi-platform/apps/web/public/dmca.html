2. DMCA Takedown Notice Requirements

Your notice must include ALL of the following information (17 U.S.C. Â§ 512(c)(3)):

  1. REQUIRED Your Contact Information: Full name, physical address, email, phone number
  2. REQUIRED Identification of Copyrighted Work: Describe the copyrighted work you claim was infringed
    • If multiple works, provide a representative list
    • Include registration number if registered with U.S. Copyright Office
  3. REQUIRED Location of Infringing Material: Provide the URL(s) of the infringing content
    • Example: https://The Musician's Index.com/user/username/post/12345
    • Be as specific as possible to help us locate the content
  4. REQUIRED Good Faith Statement: Include this statement:
    "I have a good faith belief that the use of the material in the manner complained of is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law."
  5. REQUIRED Accuracy Statement: Include this statement:
    "I swear, under penalty of perjury, that the information in this notification is accurate and that I am the copyright owner, or authorized to act on behalf of the owner, of an exclusive right that is allegedly infringed."
  6. REQUIRED Your Signature: Physical or electronic signature
    • Typed name is acceptable for electronic notices

4. What Happens After You File a Notice

  1. Receipt: We'll acknowledge receipt of your notice within 1-2 business days
  2. Review: We'll review the notice to ensure it meets DMCA requirements
  3. Takedown: If valid, we'll remove or disable access to the content within 24-48 hours
  4. Notification: We'll notify the user who posted the content
  5. Counter-Notice: The user may file a counter-notice (see Section 6)

5. Misrepresentation Warning

âš ï¸ Legal Consequences of False Claims
Under 17 U.S.C. Â§ 512(f), anyone who knowingly materially misrepresents that material is infringing may be subject to liability for damages, including costs and attorney fees.

Do NOT file a DMCA notice if:

  • You are not the copyright owner or authorized agent
  • The use is authorized (you gave permission)
  • The use may be fair use (criticism, commentary, parody, education, research)
  • You are using DMCA to suppress speech or competition

6. Counter-Notification (For Users)

If your content was removed due to a DMCA notice and you believe it was a mistake or misidentification, you may file a counter-notice.

Counter-Notice Requirements

Your counter-notice must include:

  1. Your full name, address, email, and phone number
  2. Identification of the removed content and its former location
  3. Statement under penalty of perjury that you have a good faith belief the content was removed by mistake
  4. Consent to jurisdiction in your district (or where The Musician's Index is located)
  5. Your physical or electronic signature

What Happens After a Counter-Notice

  1. We'll forward your counter-notice to the original complainant
  2. If they don't file a lawsuit within 10-14 business days, we may restore your content
  3. If they file a lawsuit, the matter will be resolved in court

7. Repeat Infringer Policy

We have a policy of terminating, in appropriate circumstances, users who are repeat infringers.

  • Strike 1: First DMCA violation - content removed, warning issued
  • Strike 2: Second violation - temporary suspension (7-30 days)
  • Strike 3: Third violation - permanent account termination

8. Fair Use

Before filing a DMCA notice, consider whether the use is protected by fair use (17 U.S.C. Â§ 107).

Fair use factors:

  • Purpose and character of use (commercial vs. nonprofit/educational)
  • Nature of the copyrighted work
  • Amount used in relation to the whole work
  • Effect on the market value of the original work

Common fair use examples: criticism, commentary, news reporting, teaching, scholarship, research, parody.
